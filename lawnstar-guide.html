🏔️ Understanding Slope Ratings

Slope is measured as a percentage — a 45% slope means the lawn rises 45cm for every 100cm forward. Most standard mowers handle up to 35%, while specialist AWD models go up to 75%.

20% 35% 50% 75% Gentle Moderate Steep Extreme
🟢
Up to 20%
Any mower handles this. Good for budget models like Worx M700 or LawnMaster.
🟡
20–35%
Most mid-range mowers. Gardena Sileno, Segway i108E, EcoFlow Blade.
🟠
35–50%
Choose Husqvarna 430X/450X or Segway H800E for these steeper grades.
🔴
50–75%
Only AWD mowers handle this. Mammotion Luba AWD 5000 rated to 75%.

🔇 Noise Level Guide

Robot mowers are measured in decibels (dB). For reference, a normal conversation is 60dB. Most robot mowers run between 53–64dB — quieter than a dishwasher.

😴

Getting Started
🔧 Setup Tips for New Owners
1️⃣
Map First
Walk the lawn boundaries with the app before the first cut. A good map = better coverage.
2️⃣
Start High
Set cutting height to maximum for the first 2 weeks. Gradually lower to your preferred height.
3️⃣
Daily Schedule
Short daily sessions beat one long weekly cut. Robot mowers work best mowing little and often.
4️⃣
Clear Obstacles
Remove toys, hoses and debris before first runs. Even AI vision models benefit from a clear lawn.
5️⃣
Blade Check
Replace small cutting blades every 1–2 months. Takes 2 minutes and keeps cuts clean and healthy.
6️⃣
Winter Storage
Store indoors over winter. Clean the deck, charge to 60% and disconnect from the dock.
